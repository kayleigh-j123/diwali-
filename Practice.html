Diwali

Diwali, also known as Deepavali, is an acient Indian festival with roots dating back to 2,500years. The name itself comes from the Sanskrit words deepa (lamp) and avali (row), translating to a "row of lamps".Historians suggest it began as an acient harvest celebration that gradually integrated into Hindu religious traditions. The festival is widely associated with Lord Rama's return to Ayodhya after defeating the demon kine Ravana, marking the victory of good over evil. While it's primarily celebrated by Hindus, it's also celebrated by Jains, Sikhs and many others globally. Today, it stands as a universal symbol of light over darkness, hope and spiritual renewal.
